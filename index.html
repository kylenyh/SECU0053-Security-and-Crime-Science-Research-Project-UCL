What is the Zynex Dashboard?

An Interactive Research Platform for Privacy Decision-Making

The Zynex Dashboard is a cutting-edge web-based platform designed to help researchers understand how people make privacy decisions in real-world scenarios. Built on the principles of differential privacy, Zynex puts you in control of your privacy settings through an intuitive interface that visualizes the trade-offs between privacy protection and system performance.

The Core Concept: Privacy Budget (ε - Epsilon)

At the heart of Zynex is the concept of the privacy budget (epsilon or ε). Think of it as a dial that controls how much "noise" is added to your data to protect your privacy. Lower epsilon = more privacy but potentially reduced performance. Higher epsilon = less privacy but better performance. Your task is to find the balance that feels right to you.

Research Overview

Outlines the focus and key buildups of the research study

Privacy Threat Model (Hypothethical)

Simplified Threat Scenario

The study models a simplified privacy threat model to evaluate how participants balance perceived privacy risks against system functionality and convenience.

Who is the Adversary?

Meta (Facebook’s parent company), a large-scale data aggregator that collects and analyses user information to personalise ads and optimise engagement.

What is the Attack Vector?

Passive profiling through metadata analysis, where user behaviour (such as browsing patterns or engagement data) is monitored without explicit consent.

Connection to Study

The privacy threat model aligns with the study’s focus by simulating a realistic scenario where users must balance privacy and system performance. Using Meta as the adversary and passive metadata profiling as the attack vector reflects real-world data collection practices, allowing the study to examine how individuals adjust their privacy preferences in response to perceived risks and usability changes.

Research Question

How do users choose the epsilon parameter when confronted with explicit privacy-utility trade-offs?

This study examines how individuals make decisions when adjusting the privacy budget (ε) through the Zynex dashboard, uncovering behavioural patterns and biases in privacy choices while examining how different negative consequences—such as slower performance, increased CAPTCHA frequency, or reduced ad relevance—influence their decisions.

Research Objectives

1

First Objective

To examine users’ decision-making when adjusting the privacy budget (ε) across different privacy levels.

2

Second Objective

To evaluate how negative usability impacts such as slower performance, frequent CAPTCHAs, or fewer personalised ads influence privacy–utility trade-offs.

Research Method

This dissertation adopts a user study approach using the Zynex interactive web-based dashboard. The platform applies the concept of the privacy budget (ε) from differential privacy, providing users with direct control to balance privacy protection and system performance.

User Interaction

Participants adjust the privacy budget (ε) while observing real-time changes in performance indicators.

Data Collection

Logs ε selections, decision time, and trade-off frequency for quantitative and qualitative analysis.

Data Analysis

System tracks latency, CAPTCHA frequency, ad relevance, and network accuracy based on ε selections.

Methodological Contribution

The Zynex platform offers a key methodological contribution by enabling interactive, real-time observation of user decision-making within an operational model of differential privacy, unlike traditional static surveys or hypothetical scenarios.

Research Limitations

Simplified Threat Model

The privacy threat model is centred on one adversary (Meta) and a single attack vector (passive metadata profiling). This omits other potential privacy risks such as data breaches or algorithmic inference.

Simulated Environment

The study provides a controlled experimental setting that may not fully replicate real-world privacy scenarios. Participants' decisions might differ when facing actual privacy threats versus simulated ones.

Participant Behavioural Validity

As the study uses a user-based approach, participants’ privacy budget (ε) choices may not fully reflect real-world behaviour due to differences in technical understanding, motivation, or context.

FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

About the Study

Learn about the research study, its duration, and how your data is protected throughout the process.

What is the purpose of this research study?

This study investigates how people make decisions about privacy settings in differential privacy systems. Your interactions help us understand the factors that influence privacy-utility tradeoff decisions.

How long does the study take?

The study typically takes between 30-45 minutes to complete. This includes reading the consent form, exploring the dashboard features, adjusting privacy settings, and completing any required forms.

Is my data confidential?

Yes, all data collected during this study is kept strictly confidential. Your responses are anonymized and stored securely. Only aggregated, de-identified data will be used in research publications.

Privacy Settings

Understand how the epsilon slider and privacy settings work in the differential privacy system.

What does the epsilon slider control?

The epsilon (ε) slider controls your privacy budget in the differential privacy system. Lower values provide stronger privacy protection by adding more noise to your data, while higher values offer better system performance with less privacy protection.

What happens when I change privacy settings?

When you adjust the epsilon slider, the system immediately recalculates the privacy-utility tradeoff. You'll see updated performance metrics showing how your choice affects both privacy protection and system accuracy.

How many times can I adjust my privacy settings?

You can adjust your privacy settings as many times as you like during the study session. Each adjustment is recorded to help us understand your decision-making process.

Technical Questions

Technical details about differential privacy, privacy-utility tradeoffs, and performance metrics.

What is differential privacy?

Differential privacy is a mathematical framework that provides strong privacy guarantees by adding carefully calibrated noise to data. It ensures that the inclusion or exclusion of any single individual's data doesn't significantly affect the overall results.

How does the privacy-utility tradeoff work?

The privacy-utility tradeoff represents the balance between protecting individual privacy and maintaining system accuracy. More privacy (lower epsilon) means more noise and less accurate results, while less privacy (higher epsilon) provides more accurate results but weaker privacy protection.

What do the performance metrics mean?

The performance metrics display the accuracy and reliability of the system at your chosen privacy level. They include measures like query accuracy, noise levels, and the expected error rate in system responses.

Data & Privacy

Information about data collection, storage, and your rights as a research participant.

What data is collected during the study?

We collect your epsilon slider adjustments, timestamps of interactions, decision patterns, and anonymized performance metrics. We do not collect personally identifiable information beyond your randomly assigned participant ID.

Can I withdraw from the study?

Yes, you can withdraw from the study at any time without penalty. If you choose to withdraw, your data will be deleted from our systems upon request. Simply contact the research team at the email provided in the consent form.

How is my data stored?

All data is stored on secure, encrypted servers with restricted access. Data is anonymized before storage and will be retained for the duration of the research project, after which it will be securely deleted or archived according to research ethics guidelines.
